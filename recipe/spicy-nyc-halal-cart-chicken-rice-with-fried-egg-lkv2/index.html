Spicy NYC Halal Cart Chicken & Rice with Fried Egg

Recreate the iconic street cart meal at home with extra heat and a fried egg on top—just $8-10 total!

👨‍🍳 Recipe by Seo the Beggar King · adapted from Moribyan's video

⏱ 45 min 📊 easy 🍽 2 servings 🔥 720 cal
At restaurant (NYC halal carts)$12
At home (total)$8-10
You save 17-33%

🥘 Ingredients

  • 12 oz Boneless, skinless chicken thighs — Bone-in thighs are cheaper; remove skin and bones yourself to save more.Amazon
  • 1 cup Basmati riceAmazon
  • ½ cup Plain yogurtAmazon
  • ½ whole LemonAmazon
  • 4 cloves GarlicAmazon
  • 1 tsp Ground cuminAmazon
  • 1 tsp PaprikaAmazon
  • ½ tsp Ground turmericAmazon
  • 1.5 tsp SaltAmazon
  • ½ tsp Black pepperAmazon
  • ½ tsp Cayenne pepperAmazon
  • 3 tbsp Vegetable oilAmazon
  • 1 tbsp White vineperAmazon
  • ½ medium Yellow onionAmazon
  • 2 whole Large eggsAmazon
🛒 Get the Ground turmeric on Amazon

📝 Instructions

  1. Marinate the chickenIn a bowl, mix 12 oz chicken thighs, 0.5 cup yogurt, juice of 0.5 lemon, 4 minced garlic cloves, 1 tsp cumin, 1 tsp paprika, 0.5 tsp turmeric, 1.5 tsp salt, 0.5 tsp black pepper, and 0.5 tsp cayenne pepper. Let sit for 15 minutes (or up to 2 hours in the fridge).
  2. Cook the riceRinse 1 cup basmati rice under cold water until water runs clear. In a pot, bring 2 cups water to a boil, add rice, 1 tsp salt, and 1 tbsp oil. Reduce heat to low, cover, and simmer for 15 minutes. Remove from heat and let sit covered for 5 minutes.
  3. Cook the chickenHeat 1 tbsp oil in a skillet over medium-high heat. Add marinated chicken and cook for 5-6 minutes per side until golden and cooked through (165°F internal temp). Remove chicken and set aside.
  4. Make the sauceIn the same skillet, add 0.5 diced onion and cook until soft (3-4 minutes). Stir in 1 tbsp vinegar and 0.5 tsp salt. Cook for 1 more minute.
  5. Fry the eggsIn a small non-stick pan, heat 1 tsp oil over medium heat. Crack 2 eggs into the pan and cook until whites are set and yolks are runny (2-3 minutes).
  6. AssembleSlice chicken and serve over rice. Drizzle with onion-vinegar sauce, top with fried eggs, and add extra yogurt if desired.

💡 Pro tips

  • Use bone-in chicken thighs for extra flavor and lower cost—just remove skin and bones after cooking.
  • Double the marinade and save half to use as a sauce for extra flavor.
  • Swap basmati rice for jasmine rice if cheaper; both work well.
  • Add a pinch of cayenne pepper for extra heat if you like spice.
  • Fry the egg in the same skillet as the chicken for extra flavor.
